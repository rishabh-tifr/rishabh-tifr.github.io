The physics behind the protocol

Cyclic denoising imports a protocol from the physics of driven disordered systems. A disordered solid sheared back and forth at a fixed strain amplitude either settles into an absorbing state — returning to the same configuration at the end of every cycle — or, above a critical amplitude, yields and keeps rearranging forever. Diffusion models show the same two phases under cyclic denoising, with the noise amplitude γ playing the role of the strain amplitude.
