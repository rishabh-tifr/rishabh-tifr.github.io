Cyclic Denoising Reveals Ultrastable Memories in Diffusion Models

Rishabh Sharma, Stefano Martiniani

New York University

Overview Supplementary Movies

We introduce cyclic denoising—repeated forward and reverse diffusion at controlled noise amplitudes—as an extraction attack for image diffusion models. Inspired by the physics of random organization in disordered solids, and unlike prior generate-and-filter attacks, it works from the model's own dynamics alone—requiring no known prompts and no access to the training set. Repeated cycling drives samples into ultrastable attractors, many of which decode to verbatim training images such as stock photographs, brand watermarks, and web-crawl artifacts, making cyclic denoising a practical auditing tool for memorization, privacy, and copyright compliance.
